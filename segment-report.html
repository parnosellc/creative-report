Segment Performance

What this is. Every ad is built from three reusable parts: a hook (the first few seconds that stop the scroll), a body (the main pitch), and a call-to-action (the closing ask). We reuse these parts across many different ads. This report scores each part on its own — not the whole ad — so we can see which hooks, bodies, and calls-to-action are actually driving sales.

Why it matters. Once we know the best parts, we can build new ads out of proven pieces instead of guessing — a winning hook + a winning body + a winning ask. More winners, less wasted spend.

How we picked the winners. A part only appears here if it was used in at least 2 different ads and produced at least 4 sales — enough to be a real signal, not luck. Hooks are scored on grabbing attention and earning clicks; bodies and calls-to-action on turning those clicks into sales (and how cheaply). Green means better than average, red means worse; the small arrow shows whether it improved or slipped in the last 7 days.

How it's laid out. Three sections — Hooks, Bodies, Calls-to-action. Bodies and calls-to-action appear twice, once for each of our two funnels, because the same part can convert differently depending on where the click leads. Tags: proven = works at scale, use more = strong but barely used (make more of it), thin = not enough data yet to trust. Click any top pick to watch the actual clip.
